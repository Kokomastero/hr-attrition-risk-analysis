# hr-attrition-risk-analysis
HR360: Workforce Intelligence & Attrition Analytics
A comprehensive HR Analytics project analyzing workforce composition, employee attrition, training effectiveness, and organizational performance across a simulated company of 3,590 employees.

Business Problem
Organizations lose billions annually to employee turnover yet most HR departments lack the data visibility to understand why employees leave, who is at risk, and what can be done about it. This project analyzes a workforce dataset to uncover the key drivers of attrition, identify performance gaps, and provide actionable recommendations for HR decision makers.

Dataset
Source: Simulated HR Dataset
Records: 3,590 employees
Tool: Microsoft Excel for data cleaning and feature engineering


Feature Engineering
To enable deeper analysis the following new columns were created from raw data —
New Column Description Age Bracket Grouped ages into 20-29, 30-39, 40-49, 50-60 Satisfaction Level Grouped satisfaction scores into Low, Medium, HighTraining Level Grouped training scores into Poor, Average, Good, Excellent Absenteeism LevelGrouped absenteeism into Low, Medium, HighPerformance CategoryGrouped performance ratings into Low, Medium, High, Excellent Promotion Percentage Calculated promotion rate across departments and job levelsSalary Band Grouped monthly income into Low, Medium, High EarnersTenure Band Grouped length of service into 0-2, 3-5, 6-10, 10+ Years

Key Insights
Attrition & Retention

1 in 3 employees left the organization — 33% attrition is critically high
Employees aged 20-29 have the highest attrition at 28.79% — young talent is leaving fastest
Port Harcourt records the highest location based attrition at 18.01%
Performance Issues is the #1 reason employees exit at 21.13%

Performance & Training

Only 966 employees are Excellent performers out of 17,414 — top talent is rare
8,158 employees have only Average training effectiveness — training quality needs improvement
43% of attrition comes from employees with 0-2 years tenure — onboarding is failing
Low satisfaction employees account for 52.74% of all exits

Diversity & Workforce

Gender is nearly balanced — 51.53% Female, 48.47% Male
Only 158 Managers for 3,590 employees — management span is dangerously wide
Average satisfaction score of 3.0 out of 5 signals a disengaged workforce
Junior level has the highest headcount at 1,105 — career progression needs attention


Recommendations

Implement Performance Improvement Plans — Performance Issues is the #1 exit reason. Structured PIP programs and regular feedback cycles are critical.
Redesign Onboarding — 43% of attrition happens in the first 2 years. A stronger onboarding and early career development program will significantly reduce this.
Investigate Port Harcourt — Location specific attrition of 18.01% suggests office culture or management issues that need immediate investigation.
Expand Management Capacity — 158 Managers for 3,590 employees is unsustainable. Promote Senior staff or hire additional Managers to reduce span of control.
Improve Training Quality — Over 8,000 employees have only average training effectiveness. Review training content and delivery methods across all departments.
